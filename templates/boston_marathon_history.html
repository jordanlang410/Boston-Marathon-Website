The History behind the Boston Marathon

Boston Strong

The first Boston Marathon was held on April 19, 1897. John Graham, being from Boston, was the first organizer of the race. He was inspired after seeing the marathon event run at the 1896 Olympic Games in Athens. Interestingly, although we are used to the typical marathon distance being 26.2 miles, the first Boston Marathon was measured at only 24.5 miles. The length of the race was not changed to 26.2 miles until 1924. The first winner of the Boston Marathon was John J. McDermott with a time of 2:55:10. The men's marathon world record today is 2:1:39 which was set at the 2018 Berlin Marathon by Eliud Kipchoge. This significant difference in time shows how far the marathon has come in the past 100 years, but also how difficult the Boston Marathon is. Famous for its "Heartbreak Hill", many who run the Boston Marathon are running it just to finish, not to break any records. Heartbreak hill is famous because runners encounter it at mile 21 and its 88 feet of elevation gain in half a mile, concours many runners. The marathon is a tradition for the city now which will take place for many years to come.
